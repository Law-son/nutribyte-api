Ghanaian Portion Size Guide

Estimate your meal sizes easily using this guide — no scale needed!

Quick Portion Estimation Methods

Use your hand as a simple guide for portion sizes:

  • Fist = ~1 cup (rice, fufu, banku)
  • Palm = ~3 oz protein (meat, fish, chicken)
  • Thumb = ~1 tbsp (oils, shito, spreads)
  • Cupped Hand = ~½ cup (beans, gari, porridge)

Common Ghanaian Foods & Portions

Food Item Typical Portion Visual Reference
Plain Rice / Jollof 1 cup = about your fist
Fufu 1 ball (tennis ball size) ≈ 200g
Banku 1 ball ≈ 250g (small orange size)
Kenkey ½ wrap ≈ 250g
Plantain (boiled/fried) 1 medium = palm size
Tilapia / Meat / Chicken Palm size = ~100–120g
Stew / Soup 1 ladle = ~½ cup
Shito 1 teaspoon = thumb tip
Gari ½ cup = cupped hand

Quick Tips

  • Stick to 1–2 fists of carbs per meal.
  • A palm-sized portion of protein is usually enough.
  • Use oils, stew, and shito sparingly — measure with your thumb or a spoon.
  • Soups and stews are energy-dense — 1 ladle per serving is a good start.
